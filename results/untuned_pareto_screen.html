Untuned architecture comparison · round 3

WH2 precode candidates — untuned Pareto screen

Expected decoding overhead against encoder throughput for 84 arms — 82 WH2 precode tuning points, the WH2 dispatch profile that wraps one of them, and Wirehair 1 as the control — each measured on the same sealed sample of about 24,390 cells and faceted across six K regions. Lower is better on y, further right is better on x, so the lower-right corner is the target.

Every arm is untuned: one uniform construction seed at every K, exactly one construction attempt, no per-K seed tables and no seed-attempt rescues. This isolates the architecture from the seed search, which is why Wirehair 1 sits so high on y — that position is construction failure, not decoding cost.

Both figures come from the same quiet single-host re-time, so the comparison is like-for-like. On this baseline WH2 trails Wirehair 1 at every K below 20,000 — starting at roughly half — and draws level in the top region, where the 4% gap is inside the measurement floor. Earlier readings above 100% were computed on the superseded 24-worker loki pass and did not survive re-baselining.

The table carries every plotted value — it is the keyboard and screen-reader path.
